Tr√ºmmelbach Falls, is a popular tourist destination known for its ten powerful, glacier-fed waterfalls contained within a mountain. Accessible via a funicular and a series of stairs and tunnels, the falls offer a unique and dramatic experience of Europe's largest subterranean waterfalls.
Unique Location: The falls are located inside a mountain, accessible via a funicular and a series of tunnels and stairs. The falls are fed by glacial meltwater from the Eiger, M√∂nch, and Jungfrau peaks, carrying a massive amount of water and debris.
Impressive Scale: The ten waterfalls cascade down at a rate of up to 20,000 liters per second, with a total drop of 200 meters. Visitors can witness the power and beauty of the falls from various vantage points, including viewing platforms and within the tunnels.
Accessibility: The site is equipped with a funicular for easier access to the higher waterfalls, but visitors will encounter wet stairs and tunnels.

Tips for Visitors:

Bring a tripod or be prepared to purchase a postcard: Capturing the moving water in photos can be challenging.
Allow ample time: Allow at least an hour to explore the falls.
Be aware of potential closures: Check for any temporary closures due to high water levels or other safety concerns.

Chapel Bridge

üìç Lucerne City Center


The Chapel Bridge (Kapellbr√ºcke) in Lucerne is a renowned tourist spot and one of Switzerland's most iconic landmarks. It is the oldest covered wooden bridge in Europe, dating back to the 14th century, and is famous for its unique design and historical significance.
It's the oldest surviving wooden bridge with a roof in Europe, originally built as part of Lucerne's fortifications. The bridge is connected to the octagonal Water Tower (Wasserturm), a historic structure that has served as a prison, treasury, and watchtower. The interior of the bridge features a series of triangular paintings from the 17th century, depicting scenes from Lucerne's history and legends. The bridge was partially destroyed by a fire in 1993 but was meticulously restored, maintaining its historical appearance.
Chapel Bridge is open to the public year-round, 24 hours a day, with no entry fee.
